## 1. What Are Cookies?

A **cookie** is a small text file that a website places on your device to remember information between visits or between pages. When we say "cookies" in this policy we include similar storage technologies — **`localStorage`**, **`sessionStorage`**, and other browser storage — that work the same way for our purposes.

Cookies can be:


- **First-party** — set by TuneNet directly through this Site.

- **Third-party** — set by a service we use, such as analytics, embedded content, or a font provider.

We also distinguish cookies by **how long they last**:


- **Session** — deleted automatically when you close your browser tab.

- **Persistent** — stored for a defined period (we list each duration below in Section 4).

## 2. Why We Use Cookies

We use a minimal set of cookies and similar storage to:


- Keep the Site running and secure (e.g., remember your consent choice);
- Remember non-essential preferences (where we add them);
- Understand how the Site is used in aggregate (only with your consent in the EU / UK).

**We do not use cookies for advertising, retargeting, or building behavioral profiles.** We do not allow third parties to set advertising cookies through the Site.

## 3. Categories of Cookies

We organize cookies into the three categories shown in our consent banner.

### 3.1 Strictly Necessary (always on)
Required for the Site to function. These do not require your consent because the Site cannot reasonably operate without them. Examples: storing your cookie-consent choice, basic security and load-balancing.

### 3.2 Functional (off by default outside the EU / UK; opt-in)
Help us remember non-essential preferences such as language, region, or form progress. The Site works without them; they just improve convenience.

### 3.3 Analytics (off by default; opt-in)
Help us understand aggregate usage — page views, referrers, rough geography, conversion to waitlist signup. We use a **privacy-respecting analytics provider** that does not build cross-site profiles. **No analytics cookies are set until you opt in via the consent banner.**

> **No "marketing" or "advertising" category exists** in our banner because we don't run any. If that ever changes, we'll add the category to the banner and re-prompt for consent.

## 5. Managing Your Choices

### 5.1 Through our consent banner
The **first time you visit the Site**, you'll see a banner asking you to choose. You can:


- **Accept all** — turn on Functional and Analytics;
- **Reject non-essential** — only Necessary cookies are used;
- **Customize** — toggle each category individually.

You can change your choice at any time by clicking **"Manage cookies"** in the footer of any page. This reopens the same panel.

### 5.2 Through your browser
You can also block or delete cookies through your browser's settings. The instructions vary by browser:


- **Chrome:** Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data

- **Safari:** Settings → Privacy → Manage Website Data

- **Firefox:** Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data

- **Edge:** Settings → Cookies and site permissions

Blocking strictly-necessary cookies may break parts of the Site (notably, we won't be able to remember that you've made a consent choice and will keep asking).

### 5.3 Do Not Track and Global Privacy Control
We do not currently respond to browser **"Do Not Track" (DNT)** signals because there is no consistent industry standard for them.

If your browser sends a **Global Privacy Control (GPC)** signal, we treat it as an opt-out of sale / sharing of personal information under U.S. state law where applicable. Since we do not sell or share personal information in any case, this changes nothing in practice but the preference is recorded.

## 6. EU / UK Users

Under the **ePrivacy Directive** (and its national implementations) and **UK PECR**, non-essential cookies require your **prior, freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent**. On your first visit from the EU / UK, no Functional or Analytics cookies are set until you make a choice in the banner. You can withdraw consent at any time — your choice is recorded with the same weight as your acceptance.

## 7. Children

The Site is intended for users **18 years of age or older**. We do not knowingly collect personal information through cookies from anyone under 18.
